Hole 18 - "Tom Morris"

Hole Overview

  • Par: 4
  • Length: 357 yards
  • Stroke Index: 16

Description

The finishing hole at St Andrews is one of golf's most memorable closing holes. Named after Old Tom Morris, this short par 4 features the famous Valley of Sin in front of the green and the iconic R&A clubhouse backdrop. The Swilcan Bridge crossing provides one of golf's most photographed locations.

Strategy Tips

  • Tee shot should avoid the right-side bunkers
  • Consider laying back from the Valley of Sin
  • The green is enormous - check pin position carefully
  • Don't forget your photo on the Swilcan Bridge!
  • Shared fairway with the 1st hole - watch for players teeing off

Historical Note

The 18th has witnessed countless historic moments, including Jack Nicklaus' farewell to professional golf in 2005 and Arnold Palmer's emotional goodbye in 1995. The Valley of Sin has played a crucial role in many championships, most notably Costantino Rocca's incredible putt in the 1995 Open Championship.
