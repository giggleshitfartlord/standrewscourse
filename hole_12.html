Hole 12 - "Heathery (In)"

Hole Overview

  • Par: 4
  • Length: 348 yards
  • Stroke Index: 9

Description

This relatively short par 4 requires strategic thinking rather than power. The Stroke bunker guards the optimal line to the green, while a series of pot bunkers protect the putting surface. The hole shares its name with the 6th, referencing the heather that once grew here.

Strategy Tips

  • Position off tee more important than distance
  • Avoid Stroke bunker at all costs
  • Green is shared with the 6th hole
  • Consider running approach using right-to-left slope
  • Watch for players crossing from 6th hole

Historical Note

The Stroke bunker earned its name from the countless strokes it has added to players' scores over the centuries. Its strategic placement demonstrates the Old Course's emphasis on position over power, a principle that has influenced golf course design worldwide.
