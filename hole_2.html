Hole 2 - "High/Dyke"

Hole Overview

  • Par: 4
  • Length: 453 yards
  • Stroke Index: 13

Description

The second hole at the Old Course presents a challenging dogleg right that demands careful strategy. Named "High" due to its elevated green complex, this hole requires precise placement off the tee and an accurate approach shot.

Strategy Tips

  • Favor the left side of the fairway off the tee
  • Beware of the Cheape bunkers on the right side
  • The green is elevated and well-protected - take enough club
  • Consider the wind direction carefully on approach

Historical Note

The second hole was originally called "Dyke" due to a stone wall that once ran along the right side of the fairway. While the wall is long gone, the strategic challenge of the hole remains intact.

Common Hazards

  • Cheape bunkers (right side)
  • Deep greenside bunkers
  • Out of bounds (right)
  • Elevated green complex

Green Reading

The second green slopes predominantly from back to front with a subtle tier in the middle section. The elevated position makes it particularly susceptible to wind effects. Front pin positions can be especially challenging due to the false front.
